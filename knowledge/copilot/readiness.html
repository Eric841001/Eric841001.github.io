Microsoft 365 Copilot Readiness Assessment Framework

Executive Summary​

Microsoft 365 Copilot is not a software deployment project.

It is an organizational transformation initiative that requires readiness across:

  • Identity
  • Security
  • Data Governance
  • Collaboration
  • Change Management
  • User Adoption

Organizations that deploy Copilot without governance frequently expose sensitive information, generate inaccurate outputs and fail to achieve expected ROI.

The objective of this assessment is to determine organizational readiness and establish a prioritized roadmap.

Assessment Framework​

Readiness is evaluated across six pillars.

PillarWeight
Identity & Access20%
Security20%
Information Governance25%
Collaboration15%
Adoption & Change Management10%
Executive Sponsorship10%

Pillar 1 – Identity & Access​

Assessment Areas​

Authentication​

Review:

  • MFA Coverage
  • Legacy Authentication
  • Passwordless Readiness

Conditional Access​

Review:

  • Device Compliance Policies
  • Location Policies
  • Administrator Protection

Privileged Access​

Review:

  • PIM
  • Break Glass Accounts
  • Access Reviews

Scoring​

ScoreDescription
1Basic Identity
3MFA Implemented
5Zero Trust Architecture

Pillar 2 – Security​

Assessment Areas​

Endpoint Security​

Review:

  • Intune
  • Defender for Endpoint
  • Device Compliance

Identity Security​

Review:

  • Identity Protection
  • Risk Policies
  • Privileged Access

Security Operations​

Review:

  • Defender XDR
  • SOC Process
  • Incident Response

Scoring​

ScoreDescription
1Minimal Security
3Security Baseline
5Integrated XDR Model

Pillar 3 – Information Governance​

Assessment Areas​

SharePoint Governance​

Review:

  • Site Structure
  • Permissions
  • External Sharing

Purview​

Review:

  • Sensitivity Labels
  • DLP
  • Retention Policies

Data Classification​

Review:

  • Confidential Content
  • Executive Documents
  • Financial Data

Key Question​

Can Copilot safely access organizational content?

Scoring​

ScoreDescription
1No Classification
3Manual Classification
5Enterprise Governance Model

Pillar 4 – Collaboration​

Assessment Areas​

Teams​

Review:

  • Team Ownership
  • Lifecycle Management
  • Guest Governance

SharePoint​

Review:

  • Information Architecture
  • Searchability
  • Metadata Strategy

Knowledge Management​

Review:

  • Content Quality
  • Knowledge Lifecycle
  • Search Experience

Key Question​

Can Copilot find high-quality information?

Pillar 5 – Adoption & Change Management​

Assessment Areas​

User Readiness​

Review:

  • AI Awareness
  • Digital Skills
  • Business Use Cases

Champion Program​

Review:

  • Champion Structure
  • Community Model
  • Reward Program

Training Framework​

Review:

  • Executive Training
  • End User Training
  • Role-Based Training

Assessment Question​

Can users successfully adopt Copilot?

Pillar 6 – Executive Sponsorship​

Assessment Areas​

Leadership Support​

Review:

  • Executive Commitment
  • Business Sponsorship

KPI Framework​

Review:

  • Adoption KPI
  • Productivity KPI
  • ROI KPI

Governance Board​

Review:

  • Steering Committee
  • Decision Model
  • Funding Model

Assessment Question​

Is Copilot strategically sponsored?

Readiness Scoring Model​

ScoreReadiness Level
0-20Not Ready
21-40Early Stage
41-60Developing
61-80Ready
81-100Advanced

Recommended Roadmap​

Stage 1​

Identity Foundation

Activities:

  • MFA
  • Conditional Access
  • PIM

Duration:

4–8 Weeks

Stage 2​

Security Foundation

Activities:

  • Intune
  • Defender XDR
  • Endpoint Compliance

Duration:

4–12 Weeks

Stage 3​

Information Governance

Activities:

  • Purview
  • Sensitivity Labels
  • DLP

Duration:

8–16 Weeks

Stage 4​

Collaboration Optimization

Activities:

  • Teams Governance
  • SharePoint Governance

Duration:

4–8 Weeks

Stage 5​

Copilot Pilot

Activities:

  • Executive Pilot
  • Champion Pilot
  • Department Pilot

Duration:

4–6 Weeks

Stage 6​

Enterprise Rollout

Activities:

  • Training
  • Adoption
  • KPI Tracking

Duration:

3–12 Months

KPI Framework​

Adoption​

  • Active Copilot Users
  • Weekly Usage
  • Prompt Volume

Productivity​

  • Time Saved
  • Meeting Efficiency
  • Content Creation Speed

Quality​

  • Prompt Success Rate
  • User Satisfaction
  • Adoption Score

Business Impact​

  • ROI
  • Cost Avoidance
  • Process Improvement

Common Findings​

High Risk​

  • No Sensitivity Labels
  • Excessive SharePoint Permissions
  • No Conditional Access
  • No MFA Enforcement

Medium Risk​

  • Weak Team Ownership
  • Inactive Sites
  • Poor Metadata

Low Risk​

  • Minor Governance Gaps
  • Training Gaps

Best Practice​

  • Assess before licensing
  • Govern before deployment
  • Protect before enabling AI
  • Pilot before rollout
  • Measure before scaling
  • Establish executive sponsorship
  • Build champion communities

Deliverables​

Typical assessment outputs:

  • Current State Report
  • Readiness Scorecard
  • Gap Analysis
  • Risk Assessment
  • Executive Summary
  • Copilot Roadmap
  • Licensing Recommendation
  • Adoption Framework

Lessons Learned​

  • Copilot success depends more on governance than technology
  • Organizations often overestimate data quality
  • Permission governance is the most common hidden risk
  • Executive sponsorship strongly influences adoption
  • Champion programs accelerate ROI
  • Purview is a critical Copilot enabler
