# Microsoft 365 Copilot Readiness Assessment Framework

## Executive Summary

Microsoft 365 Copilot is not a software deployment project.

It is an organizational transformation initiative that requires readiness across:

- Identity
- Security
- Data Governance
- Collaboration
- Change Management
- User Adoption

Organizations that deploy Copilot without governance frequently expose sensitive information, generate inaccurate outputs and fail to achieve expected ROI.

The objective of this assessment is to determine organizational readiness and establish a prioritized roadmap.

---

## Assessment Framework

Readiness is evaluated across six pillars.

| Pillar | Weight |
|----------|----------|
| Identity & Access | 20% |
| Security | 20% |
| Information Governance | 25% |
| Collaboration | 15% |
| Adoption & Change Management | 10% |
| Executive Sponsorship | 10% |

## Common Findings

### High Risk

- No Sensitivity Labels
- Excessive SharePoint Permissions
- No Conditional Access
- No MFA Enforcement

---

### Medium Risk

- Weak Team Ownership
- Inactive Sites
- Poor Metadata

---

### Low Risk

- Minor Governance Gaps
- Training Gaps

---

## Best Practice

- Assess before licensing
- Govern before deployment
- Protect before enabling AI
- Pilot before rollout
- Measure before scaling
- Establish executive sponsorship
- Build champion communities

## Lessons Learned

- Copilot success depends more on governance than technology
- Organizations often overestimate data quality
- Permission governance is the most common hidden risk
- Executive sponsorship strongly influences adoption
- Champion programs accelerate ROI
- Purview is a critical Copilot enabler
